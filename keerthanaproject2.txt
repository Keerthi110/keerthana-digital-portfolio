    <h1>History of Sir C.V. Raman</h1>
    <img src=”cv_raman.jpg” alt=”Sir C.V. Raman”>
    <p>Sir Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman, often referred to as Sir C.V. Raman, was an Indian physicist who made significant contributions to the field of science.</p>
    <p>He was born on November 7, 1888, in Tiruchirapalli, India, and passed away on November 21, 1970.</p>
    <p>Sir C.V. Raman is best known for his groundbreaking work in the field of light scattering, which led to the discovery of the Raman Effect in 1928. This discovery earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1930.</p>
    <p>He was a pioneer in the study of the interaction of light with matter and made important contributions to our understanding of the behavior of light and the molecular structure of materials.</p>
    <p>Throughout his career, Sir C.V. Raman received numerous awards and honors for his scientific achievements and was a respected figure in the global scientific community.</p>
    <p>This simple webpage provides a brief overview of the remarkable life and achievements of Sir C.V. Raman.</p>
